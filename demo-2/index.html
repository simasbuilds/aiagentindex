Demo #2: Meeting Intelligence Agent.

Turn three realistic Microsoft Teams transcripts into one reliable delivery brief: requirements, decisions, risks, stakeholder needs, and clearly owned next steps.

Live demo · transcript analysis

Convert conversation into project clarity.

Participants can open the exact source folder, build the same agent, and compare its conclusions with evidence from the three supplied transcripts.

01 · Scenario

Business problem

Important decisions are trapped in meetingsProject teams leave kickoff and discovery sessions with scattered notes, incomplete requirements, and action items that are easy to lose. This demo creates an agent that consolidates meeting evidence into a review-ready project brief without inventing missing details.

Open the Demo #2 folder ↗
03 · Starting prompt

Paste this to build the agent

Upload all three PDFs, then use this prompt. The agent is explicitly required to separate confirmed facts from assumptions and attach transcript-and-timestamp evidence.

ROLEYou are a senior Project Manager and Business Analyst specializing in meeting intelligence.
TASKReview all three Microsoft Teams transcript PDFs as one project record. Consolidate the conversations into an evidence-based delivery brief.
EXTRACTBusiness problem, current-state workflow, users and stakeholders, confirmed requirements, constraints, decisions, risks, open questions, dependencies, and action items with owners.
EVIDENCE RULEFor every material finding, cite the transcript name and timestamp. Never invent a requirement, owner, due date, approval threshold, or technical decision. Mark anything unsupported as “Not confirmed.”
OUTPUTProduce an executive summary, findings table, action register, requirements backlog, and a short list of the highest-value follow-up questions for the next workshop.
QUALITY CHECKIdentify duplicated or conflicting information across transcripts. Separate facts, reasonable inferences, and unresolved questions before presenting recommendations.
04 · Live build

Presentation sequence

  1. Open the shared folder and upload all three transcript PDFs.
  2. Paste the starting prompt and let the agent create its consolidated brief.
  3. Inspect two citations against their transcript timestamps.
  4. Ask it to separate confirmed requirements from open questions.
  5. Generate the action register and next-workshop agenda.
05 · Expected output

What good looks like

A traceable project intelligence briefThe result should surface the manual request workflow, approval delays, spreadsheet duplication, user roles, next-quarter target, monthly reporting priority, and assigned follow-ups — while clearly marking missing approval thresholds, exact dates, and technical choices as unconfirmed.
06 · Debrief

Challenge the agent

Ask the roomDid the agent distinguish a stated requirement from an inference? Could every important claim be traced to a source timestamp? Which follow-up question would reduce the most project risk? What human review is required before these outputs enter the backlog or project plan?

Verify, then trust

Open two cited timestamps live so participants see how traceability changes the quality of an AI-generated brief.

Expose uncertainty

Highlight the missing approval threshold and deadline details as examples of what the agent must not invent.

Move work forward

Finish with an owned action register and focused agenda for the next stakeholder workshop.
